To What Extent Is Violence Necessary For Change?

Background + Context

New approaches to illness prevention, treatment, and control are now possible thanks to genetic technologies. Recently, they have been employed in the diagnosis and treatment of complicated illnesses like cancer, diabetes, asthma, and heart disease. But with these advances in genetics, comes the question of how these technologies could be used for cosmetic issues, or if changes made to a gene can be passed through generations. Concerning gene therapy and genome editing, some ethical problems are: How can "bad" and "good" uses of these technologies be identified? Who determines what characteristics are normal and what qualifies as a disease or disability? Will only the wealthy be able to afford gene therapy due to its exorbitant cost?
